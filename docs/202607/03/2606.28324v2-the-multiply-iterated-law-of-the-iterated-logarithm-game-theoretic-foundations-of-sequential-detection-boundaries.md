## 摘要
任意有效置信序列和e-过程几乎无一例外地通过一个统一配方构建：在倾斜尺度上的先验上平均指数检验统计量，然后对得到的非负上鞅应用Ville不等式。混合先验设定了检测边界的宽度，通常由人工选择。我们将该配方重新解释为一个以信息为货币的双人博弈。学习者承诺一个先验；自然自适应地生成一个零均值评分过程，其难度通过累积生成函数收费来定价。学习者的混合财富满足一个沿每个实现路径成立的路径wise Gibbs变分恒等式，无需期望算子；Ville不等式、均衡条件、GROW特征以及鞍点公式都是其特例。三个信息组织其余部分。第一，迭代对数律(LIL)是这个序贯检测博弈的极小极大边界，而非任意的组合松弛。第二，最优先验不是设计选择，而是强制的均衡策略——唯一使每个边界穿越时间对自然成本相等的法则——并以闭式解形式给出精确的一阶迭代对数修正，系数为3/2 = 1 + 1/2（一个对应于Erdős基线，半个对应于鞍点周围的Laplace包络）。第三，在对数-对数尺度图中，均衡策略正好是尺度之尺度上的Jeffreys先验。Erdős-Kolmogorov积分检验是选择它的准则。两阶段有限时间LIL证明、Howard-Ramdas混合与拼接构造以及博弈置信序列均作为该均衡原理的实例呈现。配套的实证评估验证了核心恒等式，并将Erdős阈值定位于预测值。

## Abstract
Anytime-valid confidence sequences and e-processes are built almost universally from one recipe: average exponential test statistics over a prior on the tilting scale, then invoke Ville's inequality on the resulting nonnegative supermartingale. The mixing prior sets the width of the detection boundary and is usually chosen by hand. We recast the recipe as a two-player game with information as currency. A Learner commits to the prior; Nature adaptively produces a mean-zero score process whose difficulty is priced by a cumulant-generating-function charge. The Learner's mixture wealth obeys a single pathwise Gibbs-variational identity that holds along every realized path with no expectation operator; Ville's inequality, the equalizer condition, the GROW characterization, and the saddlepoint formula are all specializations of it. Three messages organize the rest. First, the law of the iterated logarithm (LIL) is the minimax boundary of this sequential-detection game, not arbitrary combinatorial slack. Second, the optimal prior is not a design choice but the forced equalizer strategy -- the unique law that makes every boundary-crossing time equally costly for Nature -- and it yields the sharp first iterated-log correction in closed form, with coefficient 3/2 = 1 + 1/2 (one for the Erdős baseline, one half for the Laplace envelope around the saddle). Third, in the log-log scale chart the equalizer is exactly the Jeffreys prior on the scale-of-scales. The Erdős-Kolmogorov integral test is the criterion that selects it. The two-stage finite-time LIL proof, the Howard-Ramdas mixture and stitching constructions, and betting confidence sequences all read as instances of this equalizer principle. A companion empirical evaluation confirms the central identities and locates the Erdős threshold at the predicted value.
