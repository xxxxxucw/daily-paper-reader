## 摘要
我们引入了一种新的共形预测方法，该方法为空间事件集合（例如热带气旋生成和地震位置）构建校准预测集。由于自然灾害具有巨大的经济影响，其预测变得越来越重要，而量化预测的不确定性对于准确的风险评估至关重要。我们的方法通过将空间点云表示为经验测度，从而能够使用（切片）Wasserstein距离对其进行评分，然后将所得分布值预测集约束为仅在训练数据流形附近有支撑。我们推导了交集集合的覆盖下界，并表明在实践中，通过简单的数据自适应选择准则，这一差距可以变得很小。由于所得的集合在解析上难以处理，我们引入了一种改进的基于流的采样过程，这使得我们能够在实践中以集成形式表示和应用这些预测集。对合成数据、热带气旋生成和地震事件的数值实验表明，我们的方法实现了接近标称的覆盖率，并且能量距离和流形距离显著低于最高预测密度区域（HDR）基线以及生成模型基线。

## Abstract
We introduce a new conformal prediction method that constructs calibrated prediction sets over collections of spatial events, such as tropical cyclone genesis and earthquake locations. Forecasting natural hazards has become increasingly important, due to their significant economic impact, and quantifying the uncertainty of predictions is critical for accurate risk assessment. Our approach works by representing spatial point clouds as empirical measures so that we can score them using (sliced) Wasserstein distance, then constraining the resulting distribution-valued prediction set to be supported only near the training data manifold. We derive a coverage lower bound for the intersected sets and show that, in practice, this gap can be made small through a simple data-adaptive selection criterion. Because the resulting set is not analytically tractable, we introduce a modified flow-based sampling procedure, which allows us to represent and apply these prediction sets in practice as ensembles. Numerical experiments on synthetic data, tropical cyclone genesis, and earthquake occurrences show that our method achieves near-nominal coverage, with significantly lower energy distance and manifold distance than highest predictive density region (HDR) baselines along with generative model baselines.
