## 摘要
我们开发了一个通用的基于设计的因果推断框架，适用于在网络化单元集群实验中存在干扰的情况，该实验通过两阶段随机化进行，且不依赖于暴露映射假设、跨集群干扰排除或伯努利处理分配。在该框架内，我们建立了对Godambe（1955）定义的线性加权估计量（LW）的完整刻画，这些估计量能够识别干扰下的各种网络因果效应。这一通用类包括若干新估计量，它们相比标准治疗概率逆加权等现有方法具有更好的理论保证和有限样本性能。对于该类中的大多数估计量，我们建立了中心极限定理和保守方差估计量，从而能够描述不同加权方案可能表现出的不同渐近行为。特别地，我们研究了集群层面的随机化如何影响各种估计量的渐近行为，并识别了一类与集群无关的LW估计量，其收敛速度独立于集群数量，并达到最优的根号N速率，其中N表示总单元数。值得注意的是，对于完全随机化，我们开发了可能具有独立兴趣的新技术，既用于建立一般依赖统计量之和的中心极限定理，也用于构造保守和偏差校正的方差估计量。我们通过广泛的模拟研究补充了理论结果，为在多种干扰结构下选择加权方法和实验设计提供实践指导。

## Abstract
We develop a general framework for design-based causal inference under interference in cluster experiments conducted via two-stage randomization on a network of interconnected units, without relying on exposure mapping assumptions, exclusion of cross-cluster interference, or Bernoulli treatment assignments. Within this framework, we establish a complete characterization of linear weighted estimators (LW) as defined by Godambe (1955) that achieve identification of various network causal effects under interference. This general class includes several new estimators with improved theoretical guarantees and superior finite-sample performance relative to existing approaches such as standard inverse-probability-of-treatment weighting. For most estimators in this class, we establish central limit theorems and conservative variance estimators, which allows us to describe the distinct asymptotic behavior exhibited by different weighting schemes potentially of interest. In particular, we study how randomization at the cluster-level affects the asymptotic behavior of various estimators, and we identify a subclass of cluster-agnostic LW estimators whose convergence rates are independent of the number of clusters and attain the optimal root-N rate, where N denotes the total number of units. Notably, for complete randomization we develop new techniques that may be of independent interest, both to establish a central limit theorem for sums of general dependent statistics and to construct conservative and bias-corrected variance estimators. We complement our theoretical results with extensive simulation studies that offer practical guidance on the choice of weighting method and experimental design under a wide range of interference structures.
